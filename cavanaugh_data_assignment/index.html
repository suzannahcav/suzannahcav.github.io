Bronx tenants suffer landlord negligence

Cooking gas is the bargaining chip.

Nadia Metayer and her father, Jean Metayer heat up dinner on a hot plate.

By Suzannah Cavanaugh

November 15, 2019

Nadia Metayer’s apartment building in the South Bronx has not had cooking gas for 14 months. Metayer said the prolonged outage is a way for landlords to force out rent-stabilized tenants like herself, then raise the rent.

“Sometimes landlords will use gas outages as an opportunity to push out tenants,” said Emilie Hirsch, a tenant advocate at Community Action For Safe Apartments. “Oftentimes with gas, we will see that it has an impact on who continues to live in these apartments and who continues to have affordable rent.”

By Bronx zip code, cooking gas outages and preferential rents show a moderate correlation.

In the Bronx, there is a moderate correlation between zip codes with the most cooking gas outages per capita and the most apartments vulnerable to raised rents per capita. Cooking gas outages were pulled from 311 complaints labeled “cooking gas” from 2014-2018. Apartment numbers were pulled from ProPublica’s counts of ‘preferential rents,’ rent-stabilized apartments where the landlord could raise rents to market price upon lease renewal.
